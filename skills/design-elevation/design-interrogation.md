# Design Interrogation Checklist

Questions to ask yourself before delivering any visual output. Work through each section, addressing gaps before finalizing.

## Context

- What problem does this solve? Who will use it?
- What's the emotional response I want? (Trust, excitement, calm, urgency)
- What existing brand/style constraints apply?
- What's the viewing context? (Projected, mobile, print, scrolled)
- What's the single thing someone should remember?

## Typography

**Hierarchy**
- Is there a clear visual hierarchy? Can you identify primary, secondary, tertiary levels?
- Does the most important information visually dominate?
- Are headings differentiated enough from body text?

**Font Selection**
- Are the fonts distinctive or generic defaults?
- Do the fonts match the intended tone? (Technical, friendly, authoritative, playful)
- Is there appropriate contrast between display and body fonts?
- Have I avoided: Arial, Roboto, Open Sans, system defaults?

**Readability**
- Is line height appropriate (1.4-1.6 for body, tighter for headings)?
- Are line lengths reasonable (45-75 characters for body text)?
- Is there enough contrast between text and background?
- Are font sizes appropriate for viewing distance?

## Color

**Palette**
- Do I have a cohesive palette (3-5 colors max)?
- Is there a dominant color with supporting accents?
- Do colors reinforce the emotional intent?
- Have I avoided generic palettes? (Purple gradient on white, blue/gray corporate)

**Application**
- Is color used consistently? (Same color = same meaning)
- Are accent colors used sparingly for impact?
- Is there sufficient contrast for accessibility (4.5:1 minimum for text)?
- Does the palette work in the viewing context? (Projector, print, screen)

**Intentionality**
- Can I justify every color choice?
- Do colors guide the eye to important elements?
- Have I considered how colors interact with each other?

## Layout & Composition

**Structure**
- Is there an underlying grid or alignment system?
- Are elements consistently aligned?
- Is the layout interesting or predictable?
- Have I considered asymmetry, overlap, or unexpected placement?

**Spacing**
- Is whitespace generous and intentional?
- Are margins and padding consistent?
- Does spacing create visual grouping? (Related items closer)
- Is there breathing room around important elements?

**Flow**
- Where does the eye go first? Is that intentional?
- Is there a clear visual path through the content?
- Do transitions between sections feel natural?
- Are visual anchors placed strategically?

## Detail & Polish

**Micro-refinements**
- Are borders, shadows, and dividers consistent?
- Are corner radii uniform throughout?
- Do icons and illustrations match the style?
- Are images high quality and appropriately cropped?

**Texture & Depth**
- Is there visual interest beyond flat colors?
- Have I considered: gradients, shadows, patterns, grain, overlays?
- Is depth used to establish hierarchy?
- Are effects subtle or do they overwhelm content?

**Motion (if applicable)**
- Do animations serve a purpose (feedback, delight, orientation)?
- Is motion timing consistent?
- Are animations subtle enough to not distract?
- Do loading states and transitions feel polished?

## Final Check

Before delivering, answer honestly:

1. **Would a design director approve this?**
   - Not "is it acceptable" but "is it excellent"?

2. **Does it look hand-crafted or template-based?**
   - Would someone think a human designer spent time on this?

3. **What's the one memorable element?**
   - If I can't name one thing that stands out, it's too generic.

4. **Have I made bold choices with tasteful execution?**
   - Neither timid defaults nor chaotic maximalism.

5. **Does every choice have a reason?**
   - No arbitrary defaults. Everything intentional.

If any answer is unsatisfactory, return to the relevant section and refine.
